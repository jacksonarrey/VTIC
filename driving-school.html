Category A
Motorcycles and Tricycles

For those passionate about two-wheeled vehicles, this category covers motorcycles and tricycles. Our experts instructors will ensure you master safe riding practices and the rules of the road, equipping you with the skills to navigate urban and rural environments confidently.

Category B
Light Vehicles

Category B is designed for light vehicles, including cars and small vans, with a weight limit of up to 3.5 tons. This category is perfect for individuals seeking personal transportation or those who wish to drive for ride-sharing services. At VTIC Driving School, we emphasize practical driving skills and road safety to prepare you for the challenges of daily driving.

Category C
Heavy Goods Vehicles

For those interested in commercial driving, Category C focuses on heavy goods vehicles (trucks) over 3.5 tons. Our specialized training covers handling larger vehicles, understanding cargo safety, and complying with transport regulations. This category is essential for aspiring truck drivers looking to enter the logistics and freight industry.

Category D
Buses

Category D is ideal for individuals aiming to drive buses and transport more than 8 passengers. Our program emphasizes passenger safety, efficient driving techniques, and customer service skills, ensuring you are well-prepared for a career in public transport.

Category E
Combination Vehicles

This category is for those who wish to drive combination vehicles, like articulated trucks and trailers. Our advanced training program will equip you with the skills needed to maneuver larger combinations safely and effectively, meeting the demands of the transport industry.

Category G
Agricultural Tractors

This category allows the operation of agricultural machinery used primarily for farming purposes. Tractors are essential for various agricultural tasks, including plowing, planting, and transporting goods on farms.
Typically varies, but regulations ensure that operators are trained to handle heavy machinery safely.

Category E
Trailers

Vehicles designed to be towed by another vehicle (articulated vehicles).
This category includes trailers that are coupled with trucks or tractors for transporting goods. Trailers can be specialized for various purposes, such as transporting goods, livestock, or equipment...
Trailers can vary significantly in weight, and the combined weight of the towing vehicle and the trailer must be considered.
